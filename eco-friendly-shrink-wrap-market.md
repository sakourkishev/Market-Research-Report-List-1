<p><p>The eco-friendly shrink wrap market consists of several biodegradable materials, each with unique properties. PLA (Polylactic Acid) is derived from renewable resources such as corn starch, known for its transparency and strength. PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates) is produced by microbes and offers excellent biodegradability and flexibility. Starch blends combine starch with other polymers to create a cost-effective, biodegradable option. Additionally, "Others" may include materials like cellulose and various bio-composites, contributing to sustainable packaging solutions that minimize environmental impact.</p></p>
