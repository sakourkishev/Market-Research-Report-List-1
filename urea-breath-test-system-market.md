<p><p>The Urea Breath Test System is a non-invasive diagnostic tool primarily used to detect Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infections in the stomach, which are linked to gastritis, peptic ulcers, and gastric cancer. This test measures the presence of urease enzyme produced by H. pylori, which breaks down urea into ammonia and carbon dioxide. <p><strong>The Urea Breath Test System Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Scintillation Technology</li><li>Isotope Ratio Mass Spectrometry</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Urea Breath Test System market encompasses two primary technologies: Scintillation Technology and Isotope Ratio Mass Spectrometry (IRMS). Scintillation Technology detects radioactive isotopes in the breath after the ingestion of a labeled urea solution, allowing for rapid diagnosis of conditions like Helicobacter pylori infection. Conversely, Isotope Ratio Mass Spectrometry measures the ratio of stable isotopes in the exhaled CO2, providing precise and reliable results for diagnostic applications. Both methods offer unique advantages in non-invasive testing and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
